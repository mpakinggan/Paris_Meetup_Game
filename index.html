Paris Meet Up

CS 180 Project by Maria Pakinggan

Project Description

My final project is a simple objective game, where the player must meet up with a person at the bottom of the Eiffel Tower.

However, Paris is a busy tourist attraction, so the player will encounter multiple obstacles, like crowds of people, on their route.

The only way to end the game is to reach your destination.

Player Controls

  • • WASD: to move the player/camera
  • • Trackpad Scroll: to look around the scene and control which direction the player goes
  • • R: to reset the game
  • • M: to change mode of game
    • Normal: any collision will only cause a slight bounce back
    • Frustrating: any collision will reset your position back to the start

Images

Objective of the game is to meet up with the person at the bottom of the Eiffel Tower.

Once the player reaches person, the animation of the player changes from walking to waving, and the game camera pans around the scene.

The walking animation used Bezier Curves to interpolate between the above keyframes.

The scene is surrounded by trees, fences, and crowds of people to build up the environment and limit the player's movement using natural boundaries.

During the game, if the player collides with anything in the scene, the player will slightly bounce back.

Graphics Technologies Used

  • • Blinn-Phong Shading with Directional Lighting
  • • Game Camera
    • For most of game, used for 1st player POV
    • At then end, used to cinematically pan over the scene
  • • Texturing
    • Skybox
    • Ground
    • Tree Models
  • • AABB - Circle Collision Detection
  • • Animation using Hierarchical Modeling
    • Scripted animation for the waving dummy
    • Interpolated between keyframes for the walking dummy
  • • Splines + Bezier Curves
    • For game camera
    • For calculating angles used in the walking animation
    • For determining the path of the walking dummies
